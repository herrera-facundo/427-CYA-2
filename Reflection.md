# Reflection

## Activity Description

For this career development experience, I participated in the University of Idaho Pitch Contest as part of my senior capstone team. The competition involved presenting a software application developed for an external client and engaging with judges over two days.

On the first day, we delivered a structured 4-minute pitch followed by a 4-minute question-and-answer session. This required clearly communicating the problem, our solution, and its potential impact within a strict time limit. Based on our performance, we placed third and advanced to the second day.

On the second day, we presented the pitch again and participated in a networking session where we delivered short, one-on-one pitches to judges. This format required quickly adapting our message and engaging in direct, professional conversations. We also exchanged business cards and discussed potential future opportunities for the project.

## Technical or Professional Decisions

A key decision was how to balance technical depth with clarity. Since the audience included both technical and non-technical judges, we focused on clearly explaining the value of the solution rather than emphasizing implementation details.

Another important decision involved structuring the pitch to fit within the strict time constraint. We prioritized the most impactful aspects of the project and ensured that each part of the presentation contributed to a clear overall narrative.

During the networking session, an additional decision was how to adapt the pitch dynamically depending on the judge’s background and level of interest. This required flexibility and quick thinking.

## Contributions

This experience was collaborative, involving my capstone team. My individual contributions included helping prepare the pitch, explaining technical components during the presentation, and actively participating in the question-and-answer sessions.

During the networking portion, I contributed by delivering concise one-on-one pitches and engaging with judges in discussions about the system and its potential applications.

## Quality Assessment

I believe my performance was strong, particularly in clearly communicating technical ideas and adapting to different formats of presentation. Placing third in the initial round suggests that the presentation was effective.

If I were to repeat this experience, I would further refine how I adjust the level of technical detail depending on the audience and improve how I handle rapid, back-to-back pitching situations.

## Career Alignment

This experience directly supported my career goals by combining technical communication with professional and entrepreneurial skills. It provided practical experience presenting a technical product, responding to critical questions, and networking with professionals.

The event also reinforced my interest in working on projects that have real-world impact and potential for continued development beyond an academic setting. Experiences like this help bridge the gap between technical skills and professional application.
