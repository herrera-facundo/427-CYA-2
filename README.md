# CYA 2 – University of Idaho Pitch Contest

## Adventure Description

For this career development experience, I participated in the University of Idaho Pitch Contest as part of my senior capstone project team. The purpose of the competition was to present our application to judges, receive feedback, and explore opportunities for funding and further development.

The event took place over two days and involved multiple stages of evaluation, including formal presentations and direct interactions with judges.

## Career Goals

My short-term goal is to work as a software engineer in areas such as artificial intelligence, systems, or cybersecurity. I aim to apply my technical background to real-world problems and gain experience building impactful software systems.

My long-term goal is to contribute to meaningful technological innovation and potentially be involved in startup ventures. Experiences that combine technical development with business strategy and communication are particularly valuable in achieving this goal.

## Context: Capstone Project

As part of my senior capstone course, my team is developing a software application for an external client. The project involves designing and implementing a solution to a real-world problem, with potential for continued development beyond graduation.

We entered the pitch contest to evaluate the project’s viability, gain professional feedback, and explore opportunities for future development.

## Pitch Contest Experience

### Day 1

On the first day, we delivered a 4-minute pitch, followed by 4 minutes of Q&A with judges. The presentation focused on clearly communicating the problem, our solution, and the potential impact of the application.

Based on our performance, we placed 3rd overall, which reqarded us with a 1,000 dollar prize and qualified us to advance to the second day of the competition.

### Day 2

On the second day, we presented the pitch again to judges. In addition, we participated in a speed networking session, where we delivered short, one-on-one pitches to individual judges in a format similar to “speed dating.”

This required quickly adapting the presentation to different audiences and engaging in direct conversations with professionals. We also exchanged business cards and discussed potential next steps for the project.
